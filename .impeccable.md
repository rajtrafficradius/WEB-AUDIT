# Traffic Radius Enterprise SEO Studio — Design Contract

## Users and jobs

- Agency administrators govern access, credentials, risky recommendations, approvals, and final releases.
- Analysts collect evidence, resolve gaps, review findings, shape the canonical action plan, and prepare client-ready artifacts.
- Client reviewers need a calm, legible review surface for evidence, comments, revision requests, approvals, and approved downloads.

## Brand and voice

- Product: Traffic Radius Enterprise SEO Studio.
- Personality: rigorous, composed, editorial, exacting, and quietly premium.
- Voice: plain English, evidence first, candid about unavailable data, never breathless or promotional.
- The interface must make provenance, confidence, recency, scope, and approval state visible wherever a decision depends on them.

## Visual direction

- Light-first editorial workspace inspired by a well-made research journal and a modern operations console.
- Warm parchment canvas, deep ink type, restrained indigo controls, copper evidence accents, and green only for verified/approved states.
- Use Fraunces for expressive display moments and Source Sans 3 for dense operational text. Provide local font fallbacks without relying on a remote CDN.
- Favor strong typographic hierarchy, generous whitespace, rules, compact data tables, and asymmetric page compositions over repetitive card grids.
- No gradients, glassmorphism, neon effects, ornamental shadows, generic AI-dashboard tropes, or decorative charts without analytical value.

## Interaction principles

1. Evidence before assertion: every metric and recommendation exposes source, as-of date, scope, rule version, and confidence.
2. Honest incompleteness: missing credentials and unavailable evidence are explicit states, never silently substituted.
3. Safe progress: destructive or externally consequential assets remain proposals with server-enforced approval gates.
4. Keyboard complete: all primary workflows, dialogs, tables, filters, and menus are operable without a pointer.
5. Reversible work: edits are versioned; state transitions are audited; retries and resumptions are idempotent.
6. Responsive density: mobile presents the review essentials; wider screens progressively reveal operational detail.
7. Motion explains: use short transitions only for state or spatial continuity and honor reduced-motion preferences.

## Accessibility target

WCAG 2.2 AA for application UI and exported HTML. Use semantic landmarks, visible focus, minimum 44px touch targets, programmatic labels, useful error summaries, accessible data-table headers, and non-color status cues.
